Challenges Facing Philippine Dairy Farms

Philippine dairy operations face unique pressures: tropical heat, remote locations, and the dual burden of chilling and heating costs.

Dual Energy Cost Burden

Dairy farms pay separately for milk chilling (electricity) and CIP/sanitisation hot water (diesel or LPG). With Karnot, the rejected heat from milk chilling produces CIP hot water at no additional energy cost — eliminating the heating bill entirely.

Post-Harvest Losses: 15-25%

Philippine dairy farms lose 15-25% of milk production to spoilage from delayed or inadequate chilling. iCOOL rapid chilling from 35°C to 4°C within 2 hours meets Grade A PMO requirements, extending shelf life and protecting farm revenue.

Milk Quality & Bacterial Count

Rapid chilling within 2 hours of milking is critical to keeping Total Bacterial Count (TBC) below Grade A thresholds. Delayed chilling in tropical 28-35°C ambient allows bacterial doubling every 20 minutes. iCOOL ensures rapid, consistent cooling.

Remote Location Challenges

Many Philippine dairy farms are in remote areas with unreliable power supply and limited technical support. AquaHERO all-in-one heat pump water heaters require minimal installation complexity and operate reliably in high-ambient tropical conditions.

Simultaneous Chilling + Heating

One thermodynamic cycle produces two outputs. The cooling side chills milk, the heating side produces CIP hot water. No energy is wasted.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does simultaneous milk chilling and CIP heating work?

When iCOOL chills milk from 35°C to 4°C, the condenser rejects heat. iHEAT captures that rejected heat and uses it to produce CIP hot water at 65-85°C. The CIP hot water is essentially free — a byproduct of the chilling process — eliminating the need for a separate boiler or electric heater.

What efficiency ratings does the system achieve?

iHEAT R290 achieves COP 4.0 for heating and iCOOL achieves EER 4.2 for cooling in Philippine tropical conditions. When operating simultaneously, the combined system efficiency exceeds either unit alone because heat rejected from cooling is recovered for heating.

Can this work in remote Philippine farm locations?

Yes. Karnot systems are designed for Philippine conditions including unstable rural power, high ambient temperatures of 28-35°C, and limited technical support. AquaHERO all-in-one units are particularly suited to smaller remote operations with minimal installation complexity.

What is HydroGreen?

HydroGreen is a climate-controlled vertical fodder production system. It uses 95% less water than traditional grazing, produces fresh green fodder year-round in a compact footprint, and achieves 21% faster cattle finishing rates. Ideal for Philippine dairy operations where land and water are constrained.

How does this reduce post-harvest dairy losses?

Philippine dairy farms lose 15-25% of production to spoilage from delayed chilling. iCOOL rapid chilling from 35°C to 4°C within 2 hours meets Grade A PMO requirements, extends shelf life, significantly reduces post-harvest losses, and increases farm revenue.
