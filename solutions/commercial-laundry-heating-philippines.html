Why Commercial Laundries in the Philippines Choose Karnot

Philippine commercial laundries — serving hotels, hospitals, uniforms, and linen rental — run thermal-intensive operations where heating accounts for 50-70% of total energy costs. With diesel at ₱119 per litre, a mid-sized laundry facility can spend ₱150,000-₱400,000 per month on fuel alone. Karnot R290 heat pumps deliver 60-75% energy savings by extracting heat from ambient air at COP 4.0-5.0, compared to a diesel boiler's effective efficiency of just 70-80%.

The transition from steam to hot water heating also eliminates boiler operator licensing requirements, annual pressure vessel inspections, and the safety risks associated with high-pressure steam systems. Karnot's EaaS model means zero capital expenditure — you pay only for hot water consumed, at a rate guaranteed lower than your current diesel cost.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much can commercial laundries save with Karnot heat pumps in the Philippines?

Commercial laundries in the Philippines typically save 60-75% on thermal energy costs by switching from diesel steam boilers to Karnot R290 heat pumps. With diesel at ₱119/L, a medium-volume laundry processing 2-5 tonnes per day can save ₱2-5 million annually. The EaaS model delivers those savings from day one with no upfront investment.

Can Karnot heat pumps fully replace steam boilers in a commercial laundry?

For most laundry processes, yes. Karnot iHEAT delivers 60-80°C hot water that meets tunnel washer, open-pocket washer, and rinsing temperature requirements. Flatwork ironers that require direct steam injection may need a small supplementary steam generator, but the heat pump system handles 80-95% of the total thermal load — delivering the vast majority of savings.

What is the payback period for a laundry heat pump system in the Philippines?

At current Philippine diesel prices of ₱119/L, most commercial laundry heat pump installations achieve full payback in 12-24 months depending on system size and daily throughput. Under the EaaS model, there is no upfront cost at all — you see net savings from the very first month of operation.

How does the EaaS model work for Philippine commercial laundries?

Under Energy as a Service, Karnot installs, owns, and maintains the entire heat pump system at zero capital cost to your laundry business. You pay a fixed rate per litre of hot water that is contractually guaranteed to be lower than your current diesel heating cost. Karnot handles all maintenance, monitoring via iMESH, and system optimization throughout the contract period.

Will the heat pump system keep up with continuous high-volume laundry operations?

Yes. Karnot designs systems with buffer storage tanks sized for your peak throughput, and multiple iHEAT units operate in cascade to deliver 10,000-50,000 litres of 60-80°C water per day. Inverter-driven compressors modulate output continuously, so the system matches real-time demand without the cycling losses common in on/off boiler systems.
