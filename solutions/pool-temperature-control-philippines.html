Why Swimming Pools in the Philippines Choose Karnot

Even in the Philippine tropical climate, commercial pools face temperature management challenges. Overnight heat loss can drop water 2-4°C, rainy season ambient temperatures cool pools below guest comfort levels, and competition pools require precise temperature control for athlete performance standards. Electric resistance heaters and diesel systems waste enormous energy maintaining these temperatures.

The Karnot iSPA inverter heat pump solves all of these problems at a fraction of the cost. With COP reaching 15.04 in Philippine ambient conditions, every 1 kWh of electricity delivers up to 15 kWh of heating — making it the most efficient pool heating technology available. The system also handles pool cooling during extreme summer heat, preventing algae growth and maintaining guest comfort. Through the EaaS model, resort and gym operators eliminate capital expenditure entirely.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much can swimming pools save with Karnot heat pumps in the Philippines?

Swimming pools and gyms in the Philippines typically save 65-80% on pool heating costs by switching to Karnot iSPA inverter heat pumps. The iSPA achieves COP up to 15.04 in tropical conditions, making it exceptionally efficient. A resort with a 100 m³ pool can save hundreds of thousands of pesos per year compared to electric or diesel heating.

Why do Philippine pools need heating if the climate is already warm?

Even in the tropics, overnight heat loss, rainy season cooling, and shaded pool areas can drop water temperatures below comfortable swimming levels — typically 2-4°C overnight. Competition and lap pools require precise temperature control (usually 26-28°C) for athletic performance. The iSPA also provides cooling during peak summer to prevent overheating and algae growth.

What pool sizes can the Karnot iSPA handle?

The iSPA range covers pool volumes from 15 m³ to 200 m³ with individual units from 6.5 kW to 28 kW. Multiple units can be cascaded for Olympic-size pools or resort complexes with multiple pools. Karnot engineers size the system based on pool volume, surface area, exposure, and local climate data to ensure optimal performance.

Can the iSPA also heat pool showers and changing rooms?

Yes. The iSPA system simultaneously maintains pool temperature at 26-32°C and provides 55°C domestic hot water for showers, changing rooms, and spa facilities. For high-traffic facilities like resort pool complexes with heavy shower usage, the iHEAT range can supplement the shower hot water supply.

Is the EaaS model available for pool heating in the Philippines?

Yes. Under Energy as a Service, Karnot installs, owns, and maintains the iSPA system at zero capital cost. You pay only for the energy consumed at a rate guaranteed lower than your current heating method. This is particularly attractive for resort developers who want to offer heated pools without the upfront investment.
