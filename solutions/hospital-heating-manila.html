Frequently Asked Questions

How much can hospitals save with Karnot heat pumps in Manila?

Hospitals and healthcare facilities in Manila typically save 60-75% on thermal energy costs by switching from diesel boilers to Karnot natural refrigerant heat pumps. With diesel at ₱119/L, a 200-bed hospital can save ₱4-8 million per year. Karnot also offers an EaaS (Energy as a Service) model with zero upfront capital cost.

What temperature range does Karnot provide for hospitals?

Karnot systems for hospitals operate across 55-80°C to meet all sterilization, patient care, kitchen, and laundry hot water requirements. The system maintains Legionella-safe temperatures throughout the entire distribution loop.

How does Karnot ensure 24/7 reliability for hospital hot water?

Karnot designs N+1 redundant cascade systems with automatic failover between units. If any single heat pump requires maintenance, the remaining units seamlessly cover full demand. The iMESH IoT platform monitors all system parameters 24/7 and alerts the Karnot engineering team before issues affect supply — enabling predictive rather than reactive maintenance.

Does the system meet Philippine DOH hot water requirements for healthcare?

Yes. Karnot iHEAT systems deliver water at 55-80°C, exceeding Department of Health requirements for hospital sterilization and infection control. The system maintains consistent temperatures at the point of use, and pasteurization cycles can be programmed to run automatically for Legionella prevention.

Can Karnot heat pumps integrate with existing hospital boiler infrastructure?

Yes. Karnot engineers design hybrid retrofit solutions that connect to existing plumbing, storage tanks, and distribution pipework. The heat pump system can operate alongside existing boilers during the transition period, ensuring zero disruption to hospital operations. Most retrofits are completed in 3-5 weeks with no interruption to hot water supply.
