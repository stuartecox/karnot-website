How Pool Temperature Control Works

A pool temperature control system moves thermal energy between the air and your pool water. In heating mode, it extracts warmth from the air and delivers it to the pool. In cooling mode, it reverses the cycle to remove excess heat — delivering 5-7 times more thermal energy transfer than the electricity it consumes.

1. Absorb Heat from Air

The DC fan draws ambient air across the evaporator coil, where R32 refrigerant absorbs thermal energy and evaporates at low temperature.

2. Compress and Amplify

The Panasonic DC inverter compressor raises the refrigerant temperature and pressure, concentrating the harvested energy into usable high-grade heat.

3. Transfer to Pool Water

Hot refrigerant passes through the titanium tube-in-shell heat exchanger, transferring heat directly to the circulating pool water safely and efficiently.

Frequently Asked Questions

What COP do Karnot iSPA pool temperature control systems achieve?

Karnot iSPA pool temperature control systems achieve COP ratings from 5.98 to 7.52 at standard test conditions (A27/W27). Under optimal conditions, COP can reach up to 14.05. This means you get 6-7 kWh of pool heating for every 1 kWh of electricity consumed.

Can the iSPA cool my pool as well as heat it?

Yes. All Karnot iSPA models feature both heating and cooling modes. In heating mode, water temperature ranges from 18 to 35 degrees C. In cooling mode, water temperature ranges from 15 to 32 degrees C. A SANHUA 4-way valve automatically reverses the refrigerant cycle.

Is the iSPA safe for saltwater pools?

Yes. All Karnot iSPA models use a titanium tube-in-shell heat exchanger that is fully resistant to saltwater, chlorine, and bromine corrosion. Titanium is the gold standard for pool heat exchanger longevity.

What size pool can the iSPA heat?

The iSPA range covers pools from 15 cubic metres up to 100 cubic metres and beyond. The smallest model (iSPA 6R at 6.5 kW) suits pools of 15-20 cubic metres, while the largest models (iSPA 39N at 39 kW) handle pools of 50-100 cubic metres. Multiple units can be cascaded for larger pools.

What refrigerant does the iSPA use?

The iSPA uses R32 refrigerant, which has a Global Warming Potential (GWP) of 675, roughly 68% lower than the older R410A refrigerant (GWP 2088) that it replaces. R32 is more energy efficient and requires less charge per unit of cooling capacity.

Can the iSPA operate in cold weather?

Yes. The Karnot iSPA operates in ambient temperatures from -15 degrees C to 45 degrees C. The full DC inverter system with Panasonic compressor and Danfoss electronic expansion valve automatically adjusts to maintain performance across this wide range.
