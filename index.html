What is an R290 heat pump?

An R290 heat pump uses propane as a natural refrigerant to transfer heat from the air to water. R290 has a Global Warming Potential (GWP) of just 3 — over 700x lower than legacy HFC refrigerants — and achieves COP ratings of 4.0-5.0 in tropical climates like the Philippines.

What are natural refrigerants?

Natural refrigerants include R290 (propane) and CO₂ (R744). They have near-zero global warming potential, are not affected by PFAS bans or HFC phase-downs, and deliver superior efficiency with COP ratings of 4.0-5.0.

How does a heat pump compare to a diesel boiler in the Philippines?

A Karnot iHEAT R290 heat pump delivers hot water at ₱3.00 per kWh of heat, compared to ₱13.86 per kWh for a diesel boiler at current Philippine prices. That is a 78% cost reduction, with zero fuel procurement, storage, or price volatility.

Does Karnot offer zero upfront cost installation?

Yes. Karnot offers an Energy as a Service (EaaS) model where we install and maintain the heat pump system at no upfront cost. You simply pay a lower monthly energy bill — typically 60-78% less than your current costs.

What COP does iHEAT achieve in the Philippines?

The iHEAT R290 achieves a Coefficient of Performance (COP) of 4.0 to 5.0 in Philippine tropical conditions. Higher ambient temperatures of 28-35°C actually improve R290 performance, meaning the Philippines is an ideal climate for heat pump efficiency.

Is R290 refrigerant safe?

Yes. R290 (propane) is classified as non-toxic with an A3 safety rating. While it is mildly flammable, modern R290 heat pumps use very small refrigerant charges (under 1kg) in sealed outdoor units, meeting all international safety standards including IEC 60335-2-40.
