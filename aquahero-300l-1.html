Frequently Asked Questions

What is the COP of the AquaHERO 300L?

The AquaHERO 300L achieves a COP of 3.48, measured at 20°C ambient with water heated from 9°C to 55°C. For every 1 kW of electricity consumed, 3.48 kW of heating energy is delivered.

How does the AquaHERO 300L differ from the 200L model?

The AquaHERO 300L offers 50% more hot water storage (300L vs 200L), a higher heating capacity of 2.4 kW vs 2.2 kW, and a faster water yield of 50 L/hour vs 46 L/hour. It is larger (Ø640 × 2002 mm vs Ø570 × 1810 mm) and heavier (~140 kg vs ~99.5 kg), making it ideal for larger households or light commercial use.

What refrigerant does the AquaHERO 300L use?

The AquaHERO 300L uses R290 (propane), a natural refrigerant with a Global Warming Potential (GWP) of just 3 — over 700 times lower than legacy HFC refrigerants. It is PFAS-free and future-proof against F-gas regulations.

Can the AquaHERO 300L operate in cold weather?

Yes. The AquaHERO 300L operates in ambient temperatures from -7°C to +43°C. At -7°C it delivers 1.00 kW heating capacity; at rated 20°C it delivers 2.40 kW; and at 43°C it delivers 2.85 kW.

What electrical supply does the AquaHERO 300L require?

The AquaHERO 300L requires a standard 220–240V single-phase supply with a maximum running current of 8.40A, connecting via a standard 13A plug — no specialist electrical installation required.
