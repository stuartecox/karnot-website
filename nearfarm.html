The Philippine Agricultural Crisis

Philippine farmers face a perfect storm: staggering post-harvest losses, prohibitive imported feed costs, 99% dairy dependency, and energy bills consuming up to 35% of operational budgets. Our livestock sector produces only 1% of domestic dairy needs while feed costs cripple profitability.

Catastrophic Food Losses

P54 billion lost annually in post-harvest waste. Up to 50% of root crops and 30% of vegetables spoil without proper cooling. 3.173 million metric tons of rice lost in 2023—enough to feed 19 million Filipinos.

Dairy Disaster

99% import dependency for dairy products. Poor seasonal grass nutrition and expensive imported feed result in abysmal milk yields. High prices keep essential nutrition out of reach for millions.

Energy Cost Stranglehold

25-35% of operating costs consumed by electricity for cooling and climate control. Frequent power outages force expensive diesel generator use. Rural areas lack three-phase power for modern equipment.

Water Scarcity Impact

Traditional fodder production consumes massive water resources while climate change intensifies droughts. Farmers struggle to maintain consistent feed supply during dry seasons.
