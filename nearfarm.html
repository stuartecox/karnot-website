The Philippine Agriculture Crisis

Decades of underinvestment, climate volatility, and dependence on imported feed have left Philippine agriculture vulnerable. Livestock farmers face mounting pressure from every direction.

Climate Vulnerability

Typhoons, droughts, and unpredictable monsoon seasons destroy crops and pasture, leaving livestock without reliable feed sources for months at a time.

Import Dependence

The Philippines imports over 60% of animal feed ingredients. Global supply chain disruptions and commodity price spikes directly hit Filipino farmers.

Water Scarcity

Traditional pasture requires enormous quantities of water. With increasing competition for freshwater resources, grazing-based livestock systems are unsustainable.

Low Productivity

Philippine cattle take 120+ days to finish. Poor nutrition, heat stress, and inconsistent feed quality keep weight gain rates well below international benchmarks.

Vertical Pasture Technology

Seeds are germinated and grown in stacked trays under precisely controlled temperature, humidity, and lighting. From seed to ready-to-feed sprouted grain in just 6-8 days -- 365 days a year.

Closed-Loop Water System

Recirculating irrigation captures and reuses water, achieving 95% savings versus open-field grazing. Ideal for drought-prone regions and island provinces across the Philippines.

Fully Automated Operation

Automated seeding, watering, lighting schedules, and harvesting minimize labor requirements. Cloud-connected monitoring enables remote management from anywhere.

Karnot Climate Control Integration

HydroGreen systems require precise temperature and humidity control to maintain optimal growth conditions. Karnot provides the full climate control stack using natural refrigerant technology.

iHEAT Process Heating

CO2 and R290 heat pumps maintain optimal germination temperatures (18-24 C) inside HydroGreen chambers. COP 4.0+ efficiency delivers heating at a fraction of the cost of electric resistance or gas systems. Waste heat recovery maximizes energy utilization.

iCOOL Precision Cooling

Transcritical CO2 refrigeration prevents overheating in tropical climates. Precision temperature control within +/- 1 C ensures consistent sprouting conditions. Inverter-driven compressors match load demand in real-time for maximum efficiency.

iSAVE Smart Platform

Cloud-connected IIoT monitoring tracks temperature, humidity, energy consumption, and growth progress 24/7. Predictive analytics optimize harvest timing. Remote management enables single-operator control of multiple HydroGreen installations.

Philippine Target Markets

The Philippines imports over PHP 30 billion in beef annually. HydroGreen-fed cattle reach market weight 21% faster with superior meat quality. Dairy operations see increased milk yield and butterfat content from nutrient-dense sprouted fodder.

Poultry and Swine

Poultry is the Philippines' largest livestock sector. Sprouted grain supplements improve feed conversion ratios, reduce antibiotic dependence, and enhance egg production. Swine operations benefit from improved digestibility and faster weight gain.

Aquaculture Integration

The Philippines is a top-10 global aquaculture producer. HydroGreen fodder can be processed into high-protein feed supplements for milkfish, tilapia, and shrimp -- reducing dependence on imported fishmeal and soybean meal.
