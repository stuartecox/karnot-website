Product Overview

Ceiling-recessed hydronic cassette fan coil units delivering whisper-quiet, 4-way air distribution for hotels, offices, hospitals, and commercial spaces.

The Karnot iZONE Cassette Fan Coil Unit range (HLC 34UE – 238UE) provides efficient, concealed climate control through ceiling-recessed installation with 4-way air distribution. Available in 2-pipe (changeover) and 4-pipe (simultaneous heating/cooling) configurations, these units deliver airflows from 340 to 2,380 m³/h and cooling capacities from 1.8 to 12.6 kW.

Designed for hydronic HVAC systems, iZONE cassette units use chilled or hot water from a central plant (such as the Karnot iHEAT R290 heat pump) eliminating refrigerant from occupied spaces. The result is a PFAS-free, quiet, and highly efficient comfort solution with 20+ year equipment life.

Operating Conditions & Technical Notes

Standard test conditions, controller features, and installation specifications

Standard Test Conditions

  • Cooling: Entering air 27°C DB / 19.5°C WB, water inlet 7°C, outlet 12°C
  • Heating: Entering air 20°C DB, water inlet 50°C, outlet 45°C
  • Water-side pressure drop measured at nominal flow rate
  • Sound levels measured at 1.5 m below unit in anechoic conditions
  • Performance data at High fan speed unless otherwise stated

Controller Features

  • Wired or wireless thermostat compatibility
  • 3-speed fan control + Auto mode
  • Heating/Cooling/Auto changeover modes
  • 7-day programmable timer (with compatible controller)
  • BMS integration via Modbus RTU or 0–10V signal
  • Condensate pump optional on all models

Installation Specifications

  • Power supply: 220–240V / 1Ph / 50Hz
  • Water connections: 3/4” BSP (34UE–85UE), 1” BSP (102UE–238UE)
  • Condensate: 25 mm OD gravity drain
  • Ceiling void: minimum 300 mm clearance recommended
  • Ceiling tile: standard 600 × 600 mm or 950 × 950 mm panel
  • Refrigerant: None — water only (PFAS-free)

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between 2-pipe and 4-pipe cassette fan coil units?

A 2-pipe cassette fan coil unit uses a single heat exchanger coil connected to one supply and one return pipe, switching between heating and cooling modes centrally. A 4-pipe unit has two independent coils (one for chilled water, one for hot water) with four pipe connections, allowing simultaneous heating and cooling in different zones from the same system.

Why choose a hydronic cassette FCU over a VRF cassette?

Hydronic cassette FCUs use water instead of refrigerant for heat distribution, eliminating the need for refrigerant piping in occupied spaces. This means no PFAS risk, 95% less refrigerant charge (confined to the outdoor heat pump), lower maintenance costs, longer equipment life of 20+ years, and compatibility with multiple heat sources including heat pumps, boilers, and solar thermal.
