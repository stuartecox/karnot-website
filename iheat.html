The 75/25 Principle: More Output, Less Input

Instead of burning fuel to create heat, iHEAT systems move existing heat from one place to another. This is why heat pumps are 3-4 times more efficient than traditional heating.

25% Electrical Input

Only a quarter of the energy comes from the grid, powering high-efficiency compressors that move heat.

75% Reclaimed Energy

Three-quarters of the energy is harvested for free from ambient air, waste heat, water, or ground sources.

100% Useful Heat

The result: 100% of required thermal energy, delivered with a 75% reduction in primary energy consumption.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the COP of iHEAT heat pumps?

iHEAT R290 heat pumps achieve COP 4.0-4.5, meaning you get 4-4.5 kWh of heat for every 1 kWh of electricity. CO2 models achieve COP 3.5-4.2 at higher temperatures up to 130 C.

What temperatures can iHEAT produce?

R290 models deliver hot water up to 75 C. CO2 transcritical models can reach up to 130 C, suitable for industrial process heating, sterilisation, and high-temperature sanitisation.

Are iHEAT systems MCS certified?

Yes, iHEAT systems are MCS (Microgeneration Certification Scheme) certified, making them eligible for UK government incentives including the Boiler Upgrade Scheme.
